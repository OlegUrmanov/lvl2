Phi Phi Island Destination

Islands off the coast of Thailand, between the mainland and the island of Phuket. Part of Krabi province. Phi Phi Islands are made up of two main islands (Phi Phi Don and Phi Phi Le) and 4 tiny islands. The islands have suffered greatly from the shocked Indian Ocean tsunami in December 2004, killed about 2 thousand people (including 1200 are still missing). The archipelago is known for shooting the movie “The Beach” with Leonardo DiCaprio in the lead role.
The name of the archipelago comes from the Malay language. In the original, it sounded like ‘Pulau Api-Api’ (Malay. Pulau Api-Api), which literally translates as “Flaming Island” or “Island of Lights”. Most likely, this name goes back to 'Pokok Api-Api' (Malay. Pokok Api-Api), or “Fire Tree”, as the Malays call Avicennia marina, which before, to a catastrophic tsunami, grew on all the islands of the archipelago.
Chi Phi Don is the main and only inhabited island of the archipelago. Area - 28 km ². Phi-Phi-Don consists of two limestone monoliths connected by a sandy slant about a kilometer in length and a minimum width of about 150 m. The main settlement is located on the spit and there is a pier on the side of Ton-Say bay that can receive small sea vessels.
